Frequently Asked Questions


SEM is paid advertising on search engines to gain immediate visibility, while SEO focuses on organic rankings that take months to develop. SEM delivers instant results but requires ongoing investment, whereas SEO builds long-term traffic but needs time to show results. Most successful businesses use both strategies together.

SEM campaigns can start driving traffic within hours of launch. You'll typically see initial data within 24-48 hours, meaningful insights within a week, and optimized performance within 2-4 weeks. Unlike SEO, which takes 3-6 months, SEM provides immediate visibility and traffic.

For local Coimbatore businesses, we recommend starting with ₹10,000-₹15,000 per month for meaningful results. This allows sufficient data collection, testing, and optimization. Smaller budgets can work but may limit reach and testing capabilities. Budget depends on industry competition and business goals.

Start with Google Ads for high-intent traffic—people actively searching for your solutions. Once Google campaigns are profitable, expand to social media for broader awareness and remarketing. Google typically delivers higher conversion rates, while social media excels at building brand awareness and capturing early-stage prospects.

Track key metrics: Cost Per Acquisition (CPA), Return on Ad Spend (ROAS), conversion rates, and overall ROI. If you're acquiring customers at a cost lower than their lifetime value and seeing positive ROI within 30-60 days, your SEM is working effectively.

  • Search Ads: Text ads on search results
  • Display Ads: Visual ads across websites
  • Shopping Ads: Product listings with images and prices for Ecommerce stores
  • Video Ads: YouTube advertising campaigns
  • App Ads: Mobile app promotion
  • Smart Campaigns: Automated campaign management
  • Performance Max: AI-driven multi-channel campaigns

Google uses an auction system considering bid amount, Quality Score (ad relevance, landing page experience, expected click-through rate), and ad extensions. Higher Quality Score can help you win auctions with lower bids, making relevance more important than just spending more money.

Quality Score (1-10) measures ad relevance, landing page experience, and expected CTR. Higher scores mean lower costs and better ad positions. A score of 7+ is good, 8+ is excellent. Improving Quality Score can reduce your cost-per-click by 20-50% while improving ad positions.

Start with phrase match and exact match for better control, then gradually test broad match for discovery. Exact match gives precise control but limited reach. Phrase match balances control and reach. Broad match can waste budget but helps find new keyword opportunities.

Keep ad groups tightly themed with 5-20 closely related keywords. This allows you to write highly relevant ads and maintain good Quality Scores. Too many unrelated keywords in one ad group leads to poor relevance and higher costs.

Monitor daily, optimize weekly, and make strategic changes monthly. Daily monitoring catches major issues, weekly optimization adjusts bids and pauses poor performers, monthly reviews involve strategic changes like new ad groups or landing pages. Over-optimization can hurt performance.

Average conversion rates vary by industry: B2B services (2-5%), E-commerce (1-3%), Local services (3-7%), Healthcare (3-6%). Focus on improving your own baseline rather than industry averages. Small improvements in conversion rate significantly impact overall ROI.

Improve Quality Score through better ad relevance, optimize landing pages, use negative keywords to eliminate irrelevant traffic, adjust match types, and focus on lower-competition long-tail keywords. Sometimes shifting to slightly different but related keywords can dramatically reduce costs.

Negative keywords prevent your ads from showing for irrelevant searches, saving budget and improving relevance. For example, a luxury product advertiser might add "cheap" and "free" as negative keywords. Regularly review search terms and add negatives to improve campaign efficiency.

Write compelling headlines that match search intent, include emotional triggers, use numbers and specific benefits, add relevant ad extensions (sitelinks, callouts), and ensure your ads directly address the searcher's problem. Test different approaches to find what resonates with your audience.

Start with monthly budget divided by 30, then adjust based on performance. High-performing campaigns should get more budget, poor performers less. Monitor impression share to identify budget-constrained campaigns.

Start with Manual CPC for control and learning, then move to automated strategies like Target CPA or Target ROAS once you have sufficient conversion data (30+ conversions). Enhanced CPC is a good middle ground. Avoid fully automated bidding until you understand your campaign performance.

Calculate customer lifetime value (LTV), then set CPA at 20-30% of LTV for profitable growth. For example, if a customer is worth ₹10,000, aim for CPA under ₹2,000-₹3,000. Factor in your profit margins and business goals when setting target CPA.

Start with 60% search campaigns (high-intent), 20% display/remarketing (awareness), 20% testing new channels. Adjust based on performance—successful channels get more budget. Always maintain some budget for testing new opportunities and markets.

Primary: ROAS, CPA, conversion rate, total conversions, revenue. Secondary: CTR, Quality Score, impression share, average position. Vanity metrics: clicks, impressions, CPM. Focus on metrics that directly impact business growth and profitability.

Install Google Analytics and Google Ads conversion tracking codes on thank-you pages, confirmation pages, or completed actions. Set up goals for form submissions, phone calls, purchases, and other valuable actions. Use Google Tag Manager for easier implementation and management.

Attribution determines which touchpoints get credit for conversions. Default "last-click" attribution often undervalues awareness campaigns. Use data-driven attribution when possible, or first-click for awareness campaigns and last-click for conversion campaigns to better understand customer journey.

Use Google's call extensions and call tracking numbers to monitor phone conversions. Set up call conversion tracking in Google Ads to measure calls lasting over 60 seconds as conversions. This is crucial for service businesses where phone calls are primary conversion actions.

Clicks are recorded when someone clicks your ad (Google Ads metric). Sessions are recorded when someone visits your website (Analytics metric). Numbers often differ due to technical issues, multiple clicks, or visitors leaving before the page loads completely.

Remarketing targets people who previously visited your website with tailored ads across Google's network. It's highly effective because you're targeting warm prospects who already know your brand. Remarketing typically delivers 2-3x higher conversion rates than cold traffic campaigns.

Create mobile-specific ad copy, ensure fast-loading mobile landing pages, use click-to-call extensions, design thumb-friendly buttons, and consider mobile-specific campaigns with adjusted bids. Over 60% of searches happen on mobile, making mobile optimization crucial for success.

Start with manual bidding to understand your campaigns and data, then gradually test automated strategies like Target CPA or Target ROAS. Automated bidding works well with sufficient data (30+ conversions per month) but manual bidding gives you more control during learning phases.

Analyze historical data to identify seasonal patterns, adjust budgets and bids accordingly, create seasonal ad copy and landing pages, and use seasonal keywords. Plan campaigns 2-3 months ahead for major seasons. Use dayparting to optimize for peak performance hours.
